# EFGL Project - Operational Pattern Analysis
**Project Period**: 13 May 2025 - 14 Aug 2025
**Scope**: 3 Floating Wind Foundations (WindFloat¬Æ platforms)
**Location**: Mediterranean Sea, France (Port-La Nouvelle)

---

## Executive Summary

Analysis of EFGL project execution reveals three distinct operational patterns that demonstrate **strong learning curve effects** and **opportunities for efficiency gains**. Key findings:

- **79.5% learning rate** with 36.88% overall improvement (F1‚ÜíF3)
- **87% reduction** in tower section completion time (15 days ‚Üí 2 days)
- **40% improvement** in per-floater preparation time (5 days ‚Üí 3 days)
- **Crane utilization**: 10.8-12.4% (significant scheduling opportunity)
- **Morning start preference** for risk mitigation and weather optimization

## Pattern 1: Morning Start Time Preference

### Summary
EFGL demonstrates a strong preference for early morning operation starts (7-11am) to allow full working day for completion if issues arise. Team also shows **opportunistic time optimization** when tasks complete early.

### Start Time Analysis

| Activity        | F1 Start | F2 Start | F3 Start | Pattern                 | Strategic Rationale                       |
|-----------------|----------|----------|----------|-------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| Tower Sec 1     | 10:10    | 11:00    | 07:30    | ‚úÖ Morning (7-11am)     | Risk buffer, full day available          |
| Tower Sec 2     | 07:50    | 09:30    | 13:50    | ‚ö†Ô∏è Mixed (F3 afternoon) | F3: Same-day optimization (see below)    |
| Tower Sec 3     | 05:25    | 07:30    | 07:30    | ‚úÖ Early morning        | Maximum daylight hours                   |
| Nacelle         | 16:20    | 16:50    | 15:20    | ‚ùå Afternoon/evening    | üîí Constrained - must follow Tower Sec 3 |
| Blade 1         | 07:00    | 12:30    | 11:40    | ‚ö†Ô∏è Mixed                | Flexible - one blade/day target          |
| Blade 2         | 06:30    | 05:40    | 08:50    | ‚úÖ Early morning        | Flexible scheduling                      |
| Blade 3         | 06:30    | 13:00    | 07:10    | ‚ö†Ô∏è Mixed                | Flexible scheduling                      |

### Key Insights

**1. Tower Sections Strategy**
- **Prefer early morning starts** (5:25-11:00)
- Allows full working day for completion
- Provides buffer time if technical issues arise
- Weather windows typically better in morning

**2. Nacelle Constraint** üîí
- **MUST be installed AFTER Tower Section 3** is complete
- Engineering requirement: No vibration damper installed
- Less control over start time ‚Üí afternoon/evening starts (15:20-16:50)
- Sequencing dependency, not a scheduling choice

**3. Blade Installation Flexibility**
- Target: **One blade per day**
- Flexible start times due to independent installation
- Can adjust based on weather windows and crew availability
- Start times vary from 05:40 to 13:00

**4. F3 Tower Section 2 - Opportunistic Optimization** ‚ú®
- Tower Section 1 F3 completed early at **12:50**
- Team seized opportunity to start Section 2 **same day at 13:50** (1 hour later)
- **Shows proactive scheduling and efficiency mindset**
- Demonstrates team's ability to capitalize on early completions
- **Not a constraint - but an optimization strategy**

**5. Overall Operational Strategy**
- ‚úÖ **Risk mitigation**: Early starts provide buffer for unexpected delays
- ‚úÖ **Weather optimization**: Morning often has better conditions in Mediterranean
- ‚úÖ **Crew efficiency**: Full daylight hours available
- ‚úÖ **Opportunistic optimization**: Capitalize on early completions when possible

### Efficiency Culture Indicator
The F3 Tower Section 2 late start (13:50) reveals an **efficiency-oriented team culture**:
- Not rigidly following "one section per day" rule
- Actively looking for opportunities to compress schedule
- Quick decision-making (only 1-hour gap between sections)
- Demonstrates learning and confidence by F3

### Controllable vs. Uncontrollable Factors

#### ‚úÖ Project Team Controls (Learning Observed)

1. **Per-floater preparation**: 5d ‚Üí 4d ‚Üí 3d (40% improvement)
   - Time from floater arrival to first tower section
   - Specific to each floater within EFGL project
   - Positioning, checks, crane setup optimization

2. **Tower section sequencing**: 15d ‚Üí 2d ‚Üí 2d (87% improvement)
   - Dramatic reduction in gaps between sections

3. **Blade sequencing**: Variable, best at F3 (consecutive)
   - F1: 4 days, F2: 7 days (regression), F3: 2 days (best)
   - Shows learning is not always linear

2. **4-day gap before Blade 1** (appears consistent)
   - Observed across all floaters
   - Possible technical/safety reason
   - Equipment/crew changeover

#### üö¢ External Dependencies (No Control)

   - F1: 11 days, F2: 9 days, F3: 11 days

   - Outside project team control

2. **Weather windows**

3. **Supply chain delays**
   - Component availability
   - Equipment maintenance schedules

## Component-Level Performance Analysis

### Component Group Breakdown

Breaking down total crane working hours by major component groups reveals differentiated learning rates and improvement potential.

| Component Group      | F1 Hours | F2 Hours | F3 Hours | Average | Learning Rate | % of Total Time |

### Component-Specific Insights

#### Tower Sections (78% Learning Rate)
- **Slowest learning rate** but **largest time component** (57% of total)
- **41% improvement F1‚ÜíF3** (25.92h ‚Üí 15.33h)
- Greatest opportunity for further optimization through:
  - Better sequencing and preparation
  - Reduced gaps between sections
  - Parallel positioning activities

#### Blades (85% Learning Rate)
- **Strong late-stage learning** with breakthrough in F3
- **F1‚ÜíF2: No improvement** (12.00h both floaters)
- **F2‚ÜíF3: 32% reduction** (12.00h ‚Üí 8.17h)
- Demonstrates that learning is not always linear
- Shows significant potential for consecutive installation (achieved in F3)

#### Nacelle (86% Learning Rate)
- **Best learning rate** despite technical constraints
- **27% improvement F1‚ÜíF3** (4.33h ‚Üí 3.17h)
- Smallest time component (11%) but most efficient
- Constrained to same-day installation with Tower Section 3 (no vibration damper)
- Demonstrates efficiency even with limited scheduling flexibility

### Steady-State Performance Analysis

**Definition**: F2-F3 average performance (excluding first-unit learning effects)

**Calculation**:
- F2 Average: 32.33h
- F3 Average: 26.67h
- **F2-F3 Average: 29.50h**
- vs F1 Original: 42.25h
- **Steady-State Improvement: 69.8%**

**Interpretation**:
- Excludes first-unit learning effects
- Represents **sustainable operational efficiency** achieved after initial learning curve
- 69.8% steady-state performance indicates significant maturity in execution processes by F2-F3
- This metric is useful for projecting performance on future floaters (Eolmed project)

### Key Takeaways

1. **Towers dominate execution time** (57%) but show **slowest learning** (78% LR)
   - Focus operational improvements here for maximum impact

2. **Blades show breakthrough potential** - 32% improvement in single transition (F2‚ÜíF3)
   - F3 achieved consecutive daily installations
   - Replicating F3 blade sequencing should be standard practice

3. **Nacelle efficiency despite constraints** - 86% LR even with same-day requirement
   - Demonstrates that technical constraints don't prevent learning
   - Team adapted well to fixed sequencing requirements

4. **Steady-state efficiency (69.8%) validates learning transfer**
   - F2-F3 average significantly better than F1
   - Indicates sustainable improvement, not one-time gains
   - Strong foundation for Eolmed project predictions
